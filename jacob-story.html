Chapter I · Who I am

Two people.
One revelation.

For a long time, I didn't know what I was doing. I knew I liked to draw. I knew I liked to solve things. But those felt like two different people, and I spent years not knowing which one I was supposed to be.

The creator and the problem-solver.
Two pulls, no clear answer.

Then came the web. Not a compromise. A revelation. The only place where both people could exist at the same time — and actually be useful together.

04
Chapter I · The Revelation

Localhost.
No internet.
No problem.

No money for a powerful PC, no internet at home. So I'd go to the cybercafé, download tutorials, go home, and work offline. Hours. Just me and a screen that wasn't even connected.

What I noticed quietly changed everything: when I touched the interface, people reacted. When I improved what was underneath, they didn't feel a thing. That asymmetry became my compass.

Madagascar → Luxembourg → France. Figuring it out as I moved.

an interface.

The question

What's here to help the user act?
What's here to reassure the designer?

Those are two very different things. Most of the time they don't belong in the same place.

A good design doesn't ask to be admired. It asks to be used.

Chapter III · Tango Luxembourg · The decision

Open.
Scan.
Understand.

I looked at how fintech apps handle it. Two seconds after opening — you already know where you stand. That became the reference.

One primary counter. A data/calls switcher. A detail page for the full breakdown.
Color-coding was removed to preserve brand neutrality — a compromise I could live with, because the structure was right.

Chapter IV · Why Hospitable

I came looking
for instructions.
I found something else.

I'd never seen a company put that kind of effort into how they welcome a candidate. One sentence stopped me. I went in looking for how to qualify — and found myself in front of real questions.

The profiles. The way the team is built across 31 countries without losing its humanity. People who seem to have their own singularity — the kind you don't often see on a team page. I caught myself wanting to know more about them before I'd even finished reading.

There were moments of doubt during this process.
But somewhere along the way I realized — whatever happens, I'm walking away with things I'm keeping for myself.

That's not something a job posting does to you.
